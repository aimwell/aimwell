• Āditta Sutta

1. Āditta Sutta.-Spoken before the Buddha at Jetavana by a deva who visited him. Like a man who rescues what he can from his burning house, let the wise man enjoy his possessions and give them away with discernment. Thus will he attain to happiness hereafter. S.i.31.

2. Āditta Sutta.-All the khandhas are on fire. Seeing this, the Ariyan disciple feels revulsion from them and, through knowledge, attains to freedom. S.iii.71.

3. Āditta Sutta.-Same as the Ādittapariyāya Sutta. S.iv.19.

Āditta Vagga.-The fifth chapter of the Devatā Samyutta of the Samyutta Nikāya. S.i.31-6.
