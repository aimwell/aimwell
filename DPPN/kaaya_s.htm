• Kāya Sutta

1. Kāya Sutta.-Mindfulness relating to the body is the path to the Uncompounded (asankhata). This the Buddha has taught his disciples. S.iv.359.

2. Kāya Sutta.-Just as the body is sustained by material food so are the five hindrances maintained by their own foods. The sutta explains what these foods are. S.v.64f

3. Kāya Sutta.-Same as above, but in reference to the seven bojjhangas. S.v.65f

4. Kāya Sutta.-There are certain things that should be got rid of by action, others by speech, yet others by mind. The sutta explains what these things are and how to get rid of them. A.v.39f
