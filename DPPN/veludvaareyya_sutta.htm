• Veludvāreyya Sutta

The brahmins and householders of Veludvāra visit the Buddha when he comes to their village and ask for a teaching which will be profitable to them. The Buddha points out to them the advantages of keeping the five precepts:

  • abstention from taking life,
  • from theft, etc.,
  • avoidance of slander,
  • harsh speech and
  • frivolous talk,
  • and of having faith in the Buddha, the Dhamma and the Sangha. S.v.352f.
