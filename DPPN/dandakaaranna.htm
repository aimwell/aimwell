• Dandakārañña

The forest which overgrew Kalinga when it was laid waste through the wickedness of King Dandakī (q.v.) (M.i.378; Mil.130).

It was on the banks of the Godāvarī and, with the Viñjhātavi, separated the Majjhimadesa from the Dakkhināpatha. It probably comprised all the forests from Bundelkhand to the river Krishna.
