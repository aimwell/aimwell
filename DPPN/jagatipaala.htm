• Jagatipāla

King of Ceylon (1047-51 A.C.). He came from Ayojjha, and claimed descent from the race of Rāma. Having slain Vikkamapandu he ruled in Rohana till he himself was slain by the Colas (Cv.lvi.13f).

He had a daughter named Lilāvatī, who later became the consort of Vijayabāhu I. Cv.lix.23f.
