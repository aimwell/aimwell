Pali Proper Names — Y

  • Yābālagāma. One of the villages given by Aggabodhi IX. for the supply of medicinal gruel to the smaller monasteries of Anurādhapura. Cv.xlix.89.
  • Yācitagāma. A ford in the Mahāvāluka-gangā. Cv.lxxii.39.
  • Yad anicca Sutta
  • Yadatthīya. A king of twenty seven kappas ago, a former birth of Pannadāyaka. Ap.i.229.
  • Yādhava. A Damila chief, ally of Kulasekhara. Cv.lxxvi.163, 173, 177.
  • Yagālla. A place in Ceylon, mentioned in the account of the campaigns of Parakkamabāhu I. Cv.lxx.105.
  • Yāgu Sutta. The five advantages of gruel: it satisfies hunger, keeps off thirst, regulates wind, cleanses the bladder, and digests raw remnants of food. A.iii.250.
  • Yāgudānānumodanā Sutta. A sutta quoted in the Sutta Sangaha (No. 61) from the Mahāvagga of the Vinaya Pitaka (Vin.i.220f.) on the virtues of giving congey (yāgu). The MT. (p.666) calls it Andhakavinda Sutta.
  • Yāgudāyaka Thera
  • Yajamāna Sutta. Sakka asks the Buddha how best to offer gifts, so as to gain great reward. The Buddha replies that the gifts should be offered to the Order of monks. S.i.233.
  • Yakkha
  • Yakkha Samyutta. The tenth chapter of the Samyutta Nikāya. S.i.206ff.
  • Yakkhasūkaratittha. A ford on the Mahāvāluka gangā. Cv.lxxii.21; Cv.Trs.i.321, n.1.
