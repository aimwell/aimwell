• Lola Jātaka (No. 274)

The story of the Bodhisatta born as a pigeon and of his friendship with a greedy crow. The story is practically the same as that of the Kapota Jātaka (No. 42) (q.v.), and was related in reference to a greedy monk who was reported to the Buddha. The crow is identified with the monk. J.ii.361ff.
