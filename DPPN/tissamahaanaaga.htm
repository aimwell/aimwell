Tissamahānāga Thera

A monk of Kutumbiya Vihāra. Having heard the Satipatthāna Sutta from Tissa Thera of Ambalena, he was on his way back when he met a wild elephant. By his virtue the elephant was subdued and Tissamahānāga took shelter from a storm under the elephant’s body. There he developed arahantship. He then went to Viyolaka-Vihāra with the elephant, and for thirty years the animal waited on him.

After death the monk’s remains were cremated at Kutumbiya Vihāra, and the elephant participated in the celebrations. Ras.ii.185f.
