• Mandāravapūjaka Thera

An arahant. In the time of Sikhī Buddha he was a devaputta, and offered the Buddha some mandārava flowers, which remained like a canopy over the Buddha’s head for seven days.

Ten kappas ago he was a king named Jutindhara (Ap.i.178).

He is probably identical with Usabha Thera. ThagA.i.219.
