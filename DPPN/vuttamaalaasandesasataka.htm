• Vuttamālāsandesasataka

A Pāli poem of the fifteenth century, containing one hundred and two stanzas and written by Upatapassī, who calls himself Sarasigāmamūlamahāsāmī. It contains laudatory verses on the reigning king, contemporary monks and several places of worship. The book is supposed to have aimed at teaching students the right pronunciation of sounds and the proper modulation of voice in reciting verses. P.L.C. 253f.
