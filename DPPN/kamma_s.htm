• Kamma Sutta

1. Kamma Sutta.-Blameworthy action of body, speech and mind, and wrong views, bring about much demerit. A.ii.252.

2. Kamma Sutta.-Seven qualities which lead monks away from ruin (aparihāniyā dhammā): abstention from delight in (1) worldly activity, (2) disputation, (3) sleep, (4) society, (3) evil desires, (6) evil friends, (7) being easily satisfied with regard to attainments. A.iv.22.

3. Kamma Sutta.-Preached at Gijjhakūta. The Buddha sees Sāriputta walking with several other eminent monks, including Moggallāna, Mahā Kassapa, Anuruddha, Punna Mantānīputta, Upāli, Ānanda, and Devadatta, and praises each for some particular quality. He ends this discourse by saying that beings of similar disposition come together. S.ii.155.

4. Kamma Sutta.-On action — new and old - the ceasing of action and the way leading to such cessation, which is the Noble Eightfold Path. S.iv.132.
