• Khata Sutta

1. Khata Sutta.-A man who praises and blames without scrutiny, who shows appreciation or displeasure without testing the object thereof — such a one carries with him an uprooted, lifeless self. Not so the man who has the opposite qualities. A.ii.2f.

2. Khata Sutta.-A man guilty of wrong conduct towards mother, father, the Tathāgata or a Tathāgata’s followers is possessed of an uprooted, lifeless self. A.ii.4f.
