• Simsapāvana

1. Simsapāvana. A grove in Alavi, where the Buddha stayed in the Gomagga and was visited by Hatthaka of Alavi. A.i.136.

2. Simsapāvana. A grove in Kosambī, where the Buddha once stayed. See Simsapa sutta. S.v.437.

3. Simsapāvana. A grove to the north of Setavyā, where Kumāra Kassapa once stayed. The Buddha also once stayed there during a journey (DhA.i.59). It was the scene of the preaching of the Pāyāsi Sutta. D.ii.316.

Simsapāvana Vagga. The fourth chapter of the Sacca Samyutta. S.v.437ff.
