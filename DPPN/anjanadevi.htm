• Añjanadevi

Daughter of Devagabbhā and Upasāgara.

When her ten younger brothers, the Andhakavenhuputtā, had conquered all Jambudīpa and were living at Dvāravatī, they divided the kingdom into ten, forgetting their sister.

Ankura, however, gave her his share and went into business. Later when all the members of her family, except Ankura, perished, she escaped destruction. J.iv.80, 84, 88, 89; PvA.111-12.
