• Gajakumbha Jātaka (No.345)

The Bodhisatta was once a minister of the king of Benares. Noticing that the king was slothful, the Bodhisatta took a tortoise as an object lesson, showing him how the indolent came to misery.

The story was related in reference to a monk who was slothful regarding his duties. J.iii.139f.
